Physical Appearance

Overall, dire wolves appeared to be more dense than agile, with greater
muscle mass and a bulkier build than other Pleistocene-era canids or
those that still roam the Earth today. However, fossils suggest that the
dire wolves’ increased weight didn’t hinder their ability to navigate
diverse terrains, hunt large prey or comfortably coexist with other
North American species. In fact, dire wolves were effective apex
predators because of their physique, not in spite of it.
